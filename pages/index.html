Affordable Medigap Plans:

Understanding Costs and Pricing

Understanding the costs associated with Medicare supplement helps you budget
effectively and choose plans that provide good value. Several factors influence premiums:

Private insurance companies use three primary methods to price policies:

  • Community-Rated: Everyone pays the same monthly premium regardless of age. These plans typically start with higher premiums but increase only due to inflation and claims experience, not aging.
  • Issue-Age-Rated: Premiums are based on your age when buying from a private insurance company. Your premium won't increase due to aging, but will rise with inflation and claims experience.
  • Attained-Age-Rated: Premiums increase as you age, typically resulting in lower initial costs but higher long-term expenses. Many prefer avoiding age-rated plans due to predictable annual increases.

What Is Medigap and Why Do You Need It?

Medigap is also called Medicare supplement insurance. It helps pay for what original Medicare doesn't cover. Medicare parts A and B are good, but don't cover everything. You still have to pay some out-of-pocket costs yourself.

If you live in Fort Lauderdale and want predictable health coverage, Medicare supplement plans can help. These standardized plans are the same regardless of which private insurance companies sell them. They help stop surprise medical bills so you can focus on getting better instead of worrying about money.

Under federal law, the government makes all Medigap policies the same. This means plan F from one insurance company has the same Medigap plan benefits as plan F from another company. This makes it easy to compare plans. You just need to look at the monthly premium and how good the health insurance company is.

Here's what original Medicare doesn't cover:

  • The annual deductible you have to pay before Medicare starts paying
  • The 20% you have to pay for most part B services and office visits
